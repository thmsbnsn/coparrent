## Purpose

These are the first-wave email drafts for the five buyers most worth contacting first.

They are meant to sound like the owner of a built product reaching out directly, not like a broker pushing a blast list.

Keep them short.
Do not attach everything on the first email.
The goal is to get the right person interested enough to say, "Send me the one-pager."

## Notes Before Sending

- Swap in a real name if you have one.
- Trim anything that sounds too polished.
- Do not add numbers you cannot defend.
- Do not attach the full package on the first touch.
- If the buyer is a clear strategic fit, keep the email even shorter.

## Bottom Line

The first wave should be simple.

You are not trying to win the deal in email.
You are trying to get the right buyer to lean in.
