## How To Use This List

This is not meant to become a giant spreadsheet of random companies.

The point is to start with the buyers who have the clearest reason to care:

- they already operate in co-parenting
- they already sell into family law
- they already work around records, scheduling, payments, or family coordination
- they already have an obvious reason to buy speed instead of building from scratch

Start with the strongest direct fits first. Do not open with pricing. Do not send the full package to everyone. Start with a short note, qualify interest, then send the one-pager and demo.

## Tier A: Best First Targets

### 1. OurFamilyWizard

**Why it belongs on the list**

- It is still one of the biggest names in the category.
- It serves both parents and family-law professionals.
- It has already shown acquisition appetite by acquiring Cozi.
- It recently partnered with Clio, which shows it is still thinking about expansion and ecosystem fit.

**Why CoParrent could matter to them**

CoParrent gives them a broader family-operations layer, a different product personality, and a foundation they could absorb, rework, or segment under another brand.

**Best outreach angle**

"You already own a strong position in co-parenting. CoParrent is a broad product foundation that could shorten time-to-market for adjacent workflows, a lighter family layer, or a second-brand strategy."

**Best titles to contact**

- CEO
- Head of Product
- Corporate Development / Partnerships

## 2. TalkingParents

**Why it belongs on the list**

- It has a strong legal-professional positioning.
- It leans heavily on unalterable records and family-law credibility.
- It is large enough to care about category expansion, but still focused enough that a product acquisition could make sense.

**Why CoParrent could matter to them**

TalkingParents is strong on communication and records. CoParrent gives them a wider operating layer: child info, docs, expenses, broader family coordination, and a different product shape beyond communications-first.

**Best outreach angle**

"CoParrent is not a clone of TalkingParents. It is a broader family coordination product with court-aware architecture that could extend your footprint beyond communication-first workflows."

**Best titles to contact**

- CEO / Co-Founder
- Head of Product
- Partnerships / Strategic Initiatives

## 3. AppClose

**Why it belongs on the list**

- AppClose is active, large, and still adding professional-facing capability.
- It already spans messaging, payments, records, and AppClose Pro.
- It clearly understands both family and legal-professional demand.

**Why CoParrent could matter to them**

AppClose may not need a basic co-parenting app. What it may care about is feature absorption, product IP, workflow ideas, or a broader foundation it can use selectively.

**Best outreach angle**

"CoParrent is a built product with real workflow depth, not a pitch deck. If AppClose is interested in product IP, feature acceleration, or adjacent platform expansion, there may be a fit."

**Best titles to contact**

- CEO
- Product leadership
- AppClose Pro / legal-professional leadership

**Best titles to contact**

- Product leadership
- Business development

## Tier B: Strong Adjacent Buyers

These are less direct, but still credible.

### 7. Clio

**Why it belongs on the list**

- It already markets heavily to family law.
- It partnered with OurFamilyWizard in late 2025.
- It clearly understands the workflow problems around family-law clients.

**Why CoParrent could matter to them**

Clio is not a direct co-parenting app buyer by default, but it could see value in a client-facing family coordination layer or in owning more of the family-law workflow outside the firm.

**Best outreach angle**

"Clio is already adjacent to this space. CoParrent is a buyer-ready product base if you ever want to own more of the family-side workflow, not just the law-firm side."

### 8. MyCase / AffiniPay

**Why it belongs on the list**

- MyCase is part of the AffiniPay family and already lives at the intersection of law practice and payments.
- Family-law workflow is already part of the broader legal software market it serves.

**Why CoParrent could matter to them**

CoParrent could be interesting if they want a client-side family operations layer to sit closer to the legal and payments stack.

**Best outreach angle**

"This is a built family-side workflow product that could complement a law-and-payments platform if you ever want a stronger family-law client layer."

### 9. Smokeball

**Why it belongs on the list**

- It actively markets family-law software.
- It is already built around workflow, documents, billing, and practice management.

**Why CoParrent could matter to them**

Smokeball could use a product like this if it ever wants to own more of the parent-facing side of family-law workflow instead of staying only on the firm side.

**Best outreach angle**

"CoParrent would give a legal software buyer a family-facing product base that it would otherwise need to build itself."

### 10. coParenter

**Why it belongs on the list**

- It is still in the category and still product-active.
- It is smaller and more directly adjacent than many legal-tech buyers.

**Why CoParrent could matter to them**

Smaller category players can be more flexible if they see a chance to pick up stronger product depth quickly.

**Best outreach angle**

"This is a full product foundation, not a loose idea. If coParenter wants to move faster or broaden its product surface, there may be a fit."

## Tier C: Buyer Profiles To Source Manually

These should be sourced manually rather than pulled from a random list:

- product studios that acquire or relaunch software
- agencies that own SaaS products, not just bill hours
- holding companies open to pre-revenue software assets
- family-tech founders looking for a head start

The fit matters more than the brand name here.

## Notes On Buyer Psychology

- Direct competitors may want the asset quietly and cheaply. Do not sound eager.
- Legal-tech buyers may need a cleaner explanation of why they should care about the parent side of the workflow.
- Smaller category players may move faster than the biggest names.
- The first goal is not to force a sale in the first email. The first goal is to confirm interest and get the right person into a real conversation.
