## What This Is

This is the outreach playbook for approaching likely buyers.

The goal is not to sound polished for the sake of sounding polished.
The goal is to sound like the owner of a real product who knows what he built and knows why it may be valuable.

## Ground Rules

- Keep the first message short.
- Do not lead with valuation.
- Do not attach a giant memo on the first touch.
- Do not oversell traction you do not have.
- Do not apologize for selling.
- Do not sound desperate.

You are not asking for a favor. You are presenting an asset that may save the other side real time and money.

## Sequence Timing

- **Day 0:** initial email
- **Day 4-5:** short follow-up
- **Day 10-12:** second follow-up with sharper fit angle
- **Day 18-21:** close-the-loop message

If someone replies with mild interest, stop the sequence and move to a real conversation.

## What Not To Say

Do not say:

- "I am open to any offer"
- "I just want to get rid of it"
- "I have no idea what it is worth"
- "It is basically finished"
- "It is the next billion-dollar company"

All of those weaken your position for different reasons.

## Tone Checklist

Before sending any outreach, read it once and make sure it sounds like:

- the owner of a real product
- someone calm, not eager
- someone who knows what is built
- someone who knows the buyer may or may not be a fit

If it sounds like a broker blast or a generated pitch, cut it down.
